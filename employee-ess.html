St. Aloysius Gonzaga Academy Faculty Policies

Review the institutional rules, leave allowances, benefits, and conduct codes governing all employees.

1. Leave Filing Policies

St. Aloysius Gonzaga Academy supports employee wellness and professional advancement with the following policies:

  • Vacation Leave (VL): Full-time academic faculty receive fifteen (15) VL credits per year. Prior approval (at least 3 days in advance) from the Department Chair is mandatory, except for seminars.
  • Sick Leave (SL): Employees receive five (5) paid sick leave credits annually. Absences extending beyond 2 consecutive days require a signed Medical Certificate verified by the school clinic.
  • Emergency Leave (EL): Five (5) emergency leave credits are provided for domestic emergencies, critical illness in the immediate family, or severe weather conditions.
  • Maternity & Paternity Leave: In full compliance with SSS Republic Act 11210, mothers receive 105 days paid leave. Fathers receive 7 days paid leave.

2. Statutory & Institutional Benefits

SAGA matches all national benefits and adds institutional support:

  • 13th Month Pay: Disbursed in full during the second week of December, computed in accordance with Presidential Decree No. 851.
  • Social Security, PhilHealth, and HDMF (Pag-IBIG): Regular monthly remittances are split between school share and employee deductions per standard guidelines.
  • PRC LET Seminars: The school provides full subsidy for mandatory Continuing Professional Development (CPD) points required for license renewal.
  • Tuition Discount: Legitimate children of regular faculty members receive a 75% tuition fee waiver at Gonzaga Academy.

3. Timekeeping & Tardiness Guidelines

To ensure standard lesson delivery, strict timekeeping compliance is monitored:

  • Daily Shift: The standard academic shift is 7:30 AM to 4:30 PM. Clock-in logs captured after 7:30 AM are automatically flagged as tardy.
  • Late Penalties: Late clock-ins reduce the net pay, calculated using the formula: Late Minutes * Minute Rate based on base salary.
  • Written Warnings: Accumulating three (3) late clock-ins in a single payroll cycle triggers a formal warning memo. Continued tardiness leads to disciplinary conferences.
